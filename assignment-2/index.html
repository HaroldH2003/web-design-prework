Clownfish

A layer of mucus on the clownfish's skin makes it immune to the fish-eating anemone's lethal sting.


  • Sienctific Name: Amphiprioninae
  • Average Length: 11 centimetres
  • Average Lifespan: 7 years
  • Habitat: Tropical coral reefs

Bright orange with three distinctive white bars, clown anemonefish are among the most recognisable of all reef-dwellers. They reach about 11 centimetres in length, and are named for the muticoloured sea anemone in which they make their homes.
